01 · The patient side

Say it when it happens.

A consultation gives you about seven minutes — and the night sweat from three weeks ago is exactly what you forget to mention. So HeyJule records your story as you live it. A check-in with Jule takes about a minute, by voice or text. Sleep, pulse and cycles sync from Apple Health on their own, and a symptom you mentioned to ChatGPT lands on the same timeline through the connector.

A minute of talking, not a stack of forms.

Tap once and speak: what happened, and how you feel about it. Jule asks the follow-up a clinician would want answered and turns the conversation into a structured note you review before anything is kept. The raw audio context is temporary — only your reviewed note is saved.

02 · Privacy

Sealed for one reader.

Your story goes to your clinician and no one else. You choose exactly which sources travel — check-ins, wearables, cycle history — and the report is encrypted on your phone, for that one clinician's key, before it leaves.

Encrypted before it leaves the phone.

The server only ever carries ciphertext; it cannot read what it stores. When your clinician opens the report, it is decrypted with their doctor-only key, on their machine. Not even HeyJule can look inside.

03 · The clinician side

A year of history, a two-minute read.

Seven minutes per patient is not enough time to reconstruct a year from memory. HeyJule spends none of it on recollection: the consult starts from a structured report where every finding links back to its source — the check-in it came from, the night of sleep, the Apple Health reading.

Decrypted at the desk, not in the cloud.

The sealed export opens in the clinician's browser with their private key. PROM scores, symptom domains and their burden appear the moment it unlocks — scored, sourced, and ready to discuss.

04 · Under the hood

Two apps, one connection layer.

A patient mobile app and a clinician web app sit on a single shared spine that moves data one way: from lived days to the desk, with consent at every step.

Gather

Apple Health syncs sleep, pulse and activity on its own; check-ins — and notes handed over from ChatGPT — capture what the numbers can't.

Understand

The conversation layer turns a minute of talking into structured symptoms, severities and timestamps — with how you felt about them kept alongside.

Seal

The patient picks the sources and the reader; the export is encrypted end-to-end for that clinician alone.

Read

The report decrypts locally in the clinician's browser: scores, trends and findings linked to source.
